Jack The Ripper

History's most notorious serial killer. Five brutal murders in Whitechapel, London 1888. A mystery that remains unsolved after 136 years. Primary sources, suspect analysis, and archival evidence.

01. The Shadow in the Fog

In the autumn of 1888, the Whitechapel district of London became the stage for a nightmare that has never truly ended. A series of murders, surgical in their precision and demonic in their brutality, gave birth to the world's first global media monster.

The streets were dark, lit only by flickering gas lamps that cast long shadows. It was here, amidst the smog and poverty, that "Jack" found his victims. The layout of the streets, resembling a labyrinth, provided the perfect cover for his escape.

02. The Letters

The name "Jack the Ripper" originates from the famous "Dear Boss" letter. Written in red ink, it taunted the police and promised to "clip the ladys ears off." While many believe it was a hoax created by journalists, it cemented the killer's name in history.

Forensic handwriting analysis has been attempted multiple times over the last century, but no conclusive match has ever been found linking it to any known suspect.
